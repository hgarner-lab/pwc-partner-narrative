## Strategic repositioning

Create a PwC version that is not simply a rebrand of the Mastercard Content Atlas.

The product should become a Human Scale thought-leadership operating system: a way for PwC marketing to aggregate approved thinking, structure the message spine, equip partners and experts, and route useful proof into the business conversations where preference is formed.

Product promise:

> One approved message spine. Many credible expert voices. Useful PwC thinking routed to the right client moments.

The app should help PwC move from content output to cultural availability:

1. Aggregate approved PwC thinking.
2. Define the global message spine.
3. Equip partners to adapt the message in their own voice.
4. Route content by client moment.
5. Measure what travels.
6. Feed learnings back into the next wave.

## Target users

1. Marketing leadership and thought-leadership teams
   - See one usable view of approved thinking, proof, messages and sector perspectives.
   - Coordinate the global argument without flattening partner voice.
   - Identify where PwC is strong, thin, repetitive or missing useful proof.

2. Sector / campaign marketers
   - Turn the message spine into sector cuts, event follow-ups, partner prompts and feed campaigns.
   - Build FS and TMT pilot activations around the Human Scale AI point of view.

3. Partners and business development teams
   - Find one credible proof point.
   - Adapt the approved message in their own voice.
   - Use a post starter, comment starter, client note or 3-5 touch nurture sequence.

## Core operating model to build into the UI

The UI should make this system visible:

```txt
PwC point of view
-> approved content base
-> partners and experts
-> client and prospect networks
-> wider business conversation
-> cultural authority / mental availability
```

The app should show that AI coordinates the rhythm behind the human layer. AI should help organise, match, summarise and suggest. It should not replace partner judgement.

### Message Spine

This is the most important new section.

It should contain a structured message object:

- Core truth
- Provocation
- Proof points
- Claims to avoid
- Priority moments
- Sector cuts
- Role cuts
- Market cuts
- Partner interpretation prompts

For the initial PwC AI prototype, seed it with:

- Core truth: AI advantage will come from human context, judgement and specificity, not more fluent content.
- Provocation: AI is making sameness cheaper; distinct expert judgement is becoming scarce.
- System claim: PwC can use AI to coordinate the system behind its people while protecting their voices.
- Measure: mental availability through culture - what leaders recognise, use and pass on.

### Content Quality

Keep the scoring system, but tune it to Human Scale.

Score each asset on:

1. Cultural availability
   - Is the idea recognisable, useful and likely to be repeated?

2. Partner readiness
   - Can a partner use it in their own voice?

3. Proof strength
   - Does it contain a usable stat, signal, story or credible claim?

4. Message-spine alignment
   - Does it support the core Human Scale AI argument?

5. Moment readiness
   - Can it be routed to a client moment, event, sales conversation or market trigger?

Labels should be practical:

- Core spine asset
- Strong proof point
- Partner prompt source
- Sector cut
- Conversation starter
- Sales follow-up
- Needs sharper POV
- Needs stronger proof
- Background only

### Source Registry

Use approved public PwC sources:

- PwC insights pages
- PwC research / report pages
- PwC blog or newsroom pages
- PwC industry pages
- PwC service/topic pages
- Event pages and sponsorship content where approved

Do not show developer-oriented crawler controls in the product surface.

Do not implement public LinkedIn scraping. Partner and C-level perspectives should come from approved PwC website content, partner-authored thought leadership, research pages, blogs, event pages or newsroom content.

## Acceptance checklist

The PwC Human Scale version is acceptable when:

- The app opens without visual regression.
- Branding and language are PwC / Human Scale, not Mastercard.
- The homepage frames the system around cultural availability and partner-led activation.
- The nav includes Message Spine and Partner Activation.
- Content Quality is activation-oriented, not web-ops oriented.
- Message Spine shows core truth, provocation, proof, claims to avoid and priority moments.

- Campaign Builder creates a 3-5 touch partner/feed/nurture activation sequence.

- Every generated output uses one proof point and cites the source asset.
- The app includes Moments that Matter mapping.
